## Ciriola

![](https://media.lacucinaitaliana.com/photos/5e07aacdbc15b2000845ac7d/2:1/w_1200,c_limit/Ciriola.jpg)

Based on the recipe by [La Cucina Italiana](https://www.lacucinaitaliana.com/italian-food/italian-dishes/ciriola-roman-bread-made-in-lazio). This flour mix mimics Italian tipo 0 W 360.



### Ingredients

#### Biga

- 220 g King Arthur bread flour
- 20 g King Arthur whole wheat flour 
- 135 g water
- 1/2 tsp instant dry yeast

#### Dough

- 220 g King Arthur bread flour
- 20 g King Arthur whole wheat flour
- 203 g water
- 15 g salt



### Procedure

- [ ] Dissolve the yeast in water at room temperature.
- [ ] Add the flour, sifting in the whole wheat flour to remove bran, and knead the dough. 
- [ ] Rest for 12 to 18 hours at about 65°F, covered with a damp cloth.
- [ ] To make the dough, add the next amount of water, flour and salt. Again, sift in the whole wheat flour.
- [ ] Mix into a uniform dough.
- [ ] Rest for 1 hour, covered with a damp cloth.
- [ ] Measure out 100-g rolls. 
- [ ] Roll and form into the traditional spindle shape.
- [ ] Lay then out on a baguette tray covered in parchment paper. 
- [ ] Cut lengthwise
- [ ] Rest for 30 minutes.
- [ ] Dust with flour and bake at 355°F for 20 minutes. Add a ramekin of water to the oven for the first 15 minutes.
- [ ] Cool on a wire rack.
