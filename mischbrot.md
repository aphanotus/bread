## Mischbrot 

A 50% rye flour bread with a thick crust

### Ingredients

- **100g** Active sourdough starter
- **200g** Bread flour
- **250g** Rye flour (medium rye is best)
- **360g** Water (Lukewarm)
- **10g** Kosher salt

### Procedure

**1. Mix (Expect Paste, Not Dough)** In a large bowl, whisk the active starter and water together until it looks like milky water. Add the bread flour, rye flour, and salt. Mix thoroughly with a sturdy spoon or your hands. It will feel like thick, sticky clay or wet concrete. This is exactly right. Cover the bowl.

**2.Bulk Fermentation (The Watchful Eye)** Rye ferments much faster than wheat. Let the bowl sit at room temperature for about 3 to 4 hours. You are looking for the dough to increase in volume by about 50% and look aerated and puffy.

**3. Shaping** Use a banneton (or line a medium bowl or with a clean kitchen towel). Dust heavily with flour (rice flour is best if you have it, otherwise use a generous amount of bread flour). Dip your hands in a bowl of water to keep the dough from sticking to you. Gently scoop the puffy dough out of its bowl and pass it back and forth between your wet hands, gently patting and smoothing it into a round mound. Drop this mound directly into your heavily floured, towel-lined bowl.

**4. Final Proof** Cover the proofing bowl and let it sit at room temperature for 1 to 2 hours. You will know it is ready when the surface of the dough shows slight "spiderweb" cracks—this is the classic signature of rye expanding. During the last 45 minutes of this proof, place your Dutch oven (with the lid on) into your oven and preheat to 450°F.

**5. Transfer and Score** Cut a piece of parchment paper. Place the parchment over the top of your proofing bowl, put a flat plate or cutting board over that, and quickly invert the whole thing. Lift off the bowl and peel back the towel. Your dough is now sitting on the parchment. Score the top with a simple cross using a wet blade.

**6. Dutch Oven Bake** Using the parchment paper as a sling, carefully lower the dough into the screaming hot Dutch oven. Put the lid on immediately. Bake covered for 25 minutes. Carefully remove the hot lid, lower the oven temperature to 425°F, and bake for an additional 20 to 25 minutes until the crust is deeply browned and thick.

**7. Cooling** Immediately remove the loaf from the Dutch oven and place it on a wire cooling rack. Rye bread retains moisture far longer than wheat bread. It must cool for an absolute minimum of 12 hours (overnight is best) before you slice into it. Cutting it early will result in a sticky, gummy center.
